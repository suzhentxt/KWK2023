Data visualisation, when combined with comprehensive data collection and analysis, can help expose the pink tax and its impact on consumers. By making price disparities more visible and understandable, data visualization can drive consumer empowerment, policy changes, and industry transparency, ultimately working towards reducing or eliminating the pink tax.
Data visualization can contribute to addressing the problem of the pink tax, which refers to the higher prices often charged for products marketed towards women compared to similar products for men.
PRODUCT CATEGORISATION
Data visualization can categorize products based on their gendered marketing and price differences. Visual representations can highlight the prevalence of the pink tax across various product categories, such as personal care items, clothing, and toys, making the issue more apparent and understandable
Data visualisation can present clear and visual comparisons of prices for similar products marketed towards different genders. By visualising price discrepancies, consumers can easily see the differences and become more aware of the pink tax

TREND ANALYSIS
Data visualization can track price trends over time, allowing consumers and researchers to identify patterns and changes in the pink tax. Visualizing historical data can provide insights into whether the pink tax is increasing or decreasing, helping to assess the effectiveness of advocacy efforts and policy changes.
CORPORATE TRANSPARENCY
Data visualisation can encourage companies to be more transparent about their pricing practices. By visualising and comparing prices across different brands, consumers can make informed choices and support companies that offer gender-neutral pricing or more equitable pricing practices.
PUBLIC AWARENESS AND EDUCATION
Data visualization can help educate the public about the pink tax and its implications. Through visually engaging presentations, infographics, and social media campaigns, data visualization can raise awareness about the issue, generate conversations, and promote a more equitable marketplace.
